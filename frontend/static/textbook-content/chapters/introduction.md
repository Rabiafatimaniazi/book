# Introduction to Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

## What is Physical AI?

Physical AI is an emerging field that combines artificial intelligence with physical systems. Unlike traditional AI that operates primarily in digital spaces, Physical AI interacts with the real world through robots, sensors, and actuators. This creates unique challenges and opportunities:

- **Real-time constraints**: Physical systems must respond quickly to environmental changes
- **Uncertainty handling**: The real world is noisy and unpredictable
- **Embodiment**: The physical form of a system affects its capabilities and limitations

## The Rise of Humanoid Robots

Humanoid robots, designed with human-like form and capabilities, represent one of the most ambitious goals in robotics. These systems aim to:

- Navigate human environments effectively
- Interact naturally with humans
- Perform tasks designed for human bodies
- Serve as platforms for understanding human cognition

## Key Challenges in Physical AI

Building effective Physical AI systems requires addressing several fundamental challenges:

### Perception in Dynamic Environments
Robots must continuously interpret sensory data to understand their environment, which changes constantly and often unpredictably.

### Real-time Decision Making
Physical systems must make decisions quickly to avoid falling, colliding, or failing to complete tasks safely.

### Learning from Physical Interaction
Unlike digital AI systems, Physical AI learns through direct interaction with the physical world, which can be costly and time-consuming.
